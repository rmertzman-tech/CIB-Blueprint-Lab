Student Field Guide: The Ethics of Geometry

1. Key Concepts (Definitions)

The CIB (The Circles): You are not a point; you are a hierarchy. The Gold Core is your biological survival (Roots). The Blue Ring is your culture/habits (Trunk). [cite_start]The Green Outer Ring is your "Leading Edge"—your ability to make complex, manual choices[cite: 341].

Truncation (The Crash): When stress gets too high, you can't afford to be complex. The system "sheds" layers to save energy. You lose the Green Ring first (Agency), then the Blue (Culture), until only the Gold (Survival) remains. [cite_start]This is the Sapolsky Limit in action[cite: 192].

BioBond (The Cooling System): This button represents "Social Scaffolding"—things like healthcare, supportive laws, or community trust. It doesn't give you energy directly; it lowers the temperature of the environment, [cite_start]making it cheaper for you to maintain your "Green Ring"[cite: 280].

2. Experiments to Try

Experiment A: The Rust Belt Scenario
1. Set Entropy to High (0.9+).
2. Set Budget to Low (2.0).
3. Observe: Watch the agents "Truncate" (shrink). They lose their Green Rings (Agency) and turn Red (Stressed). They are now in "Survival Mode."
Experiment B: The "BioBond" Rescue
1. Keep the settings from Experiment A (Agents are dying/shrinking).
2. Click ACTIVATE BIOBOND.
3. Observe: The red glow fades. The Green Rings slowly grow back. You didn't change the agents; you changed their environment, allowing their "Leading Edge" to return.
Experiment C: The Social Contract Test
1. Switch to Hobbes. Notice how stress drops (Order), but growth stops (Control).
2. Switch to Nozick. Notice how freedom maximizes growth, but if Entropy spikes, weak agents crash instantly.
